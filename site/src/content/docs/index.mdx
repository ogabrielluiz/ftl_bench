`ftl_bench` evaluates agents through a game: a solo, long-horizon one. Your model plays
*FTL: Faster Than Light* turn by turn, managing power, crew and weapons; fighting, fleeing and
surviving events; jumping across a branching map toward the rebel flagship. The harness scores
how far it gets. There is no scripted policy in the way.

<CardGrid stagger>
	<Card title="Bring your own model or agent" icon="rocket">
		Plug a model in as a one-function backend, or wire your whole agent around the env. The
		same harness drives everything, so results stay comparable. See
		[Bring your model or agent](/evaluate/bring-your-model/).
	</Card>
	<Card title="Resists saturation" icon="seti:lock">
		FTL is a long sequence of interdependent decisions under uncertainty: resource management,
		combat micro, risk, and planning across sectors. There is no single right move and the
		ceiling is high.
	</Card>
	<Card title="Scored by goal achievement" icon="approve-check">
		Goal-conditioned partial credit with a legitimacy gate. Headline metric **GCS@1**, a strict
		solve rate, and random plus scripted baselines for context.
	</Card>
	<Card title="Reproducible and transparent" icon="document">
		Seed-pinned runs, held-out tiers, and a full trajectory plus manifest logged for every run.
	</Card>
</CardGrid>
